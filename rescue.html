Rescue Stories

Story of Daisy






Daisy was found in the worst condition as she was severely infected by Maggots when she was rescued by our team.

Myiasis/maggots is the medical term for an infestation of fly larvae, either maggots or grubs, on the skin of a living animal. Dogs with festering wounds, chronic skin problems, or mobility impairment issues are more likely to develop this disease. This is a serious condition and the removal of the maggots or grubs should be handled by a veterinary professional in order to avoid the release of dangerous enzymes and toxins into the dog’s bloodstream.


We kept our hopes high and fingers crossed. The recovery phase wasn't easy at all but now after 1.5 months she is perfectly fine and happy. We are currently fostering her at our place and it makes our heart shine to see her jumping around all the time.

Story of Rustom






It took more than 3 days to rescue this gentle soul.As it is quite visible from the picture, this was quite difficult and challenging for our team.

His flesh from the entire neck area was missing, and it was impossible to tie anything to get hold of him. We stay away from using any sedatives which made our task even more herculean. We observed him for 2 hours each day, because most of the time we couldn't spot him on the road as his fellow mates kept him away and people threw stones at him, as his wound smelled very foul. Finally, we rescued him by using chest belt, muzzle and then a leash over it. After that we took him to the Doctor, who told us all the medication required.

It took him quite some time to heal. But recently during the start of December, we saw his jaw bleeding mercilessly. Our team has been fully united to raise maximum funds for the poor soul and he is under treatment as of now.
